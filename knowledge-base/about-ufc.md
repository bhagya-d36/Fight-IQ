# About the UFC

## What is the UFC?

The UFC (Ultimate Fighting Championship) is the largest and most prominent
mixed martial arts (MMA) promotion in the world. Founded in 1993, it hosts
professional MMA fights across weight divisions for both men and women, and
crowns a champion in each division.

## What is MMA?

MMA (Mixed Martial Arts) is a full-contact combat sport that allows a wide
range of fighting techniques from disciplines such as boxing, wrestling,
Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, Muay Thai, karate, judo, and kickboxing, combining
striking and grappling into a single contest.

## Who owns the UFC?

The UFC is owned by TKO Group Holdings, a company majority-owned by Endeavor
that also owns WWE. TKO Group was formed in 2023 when Endeavor merged its UFC
and WWE assets. Dana White serves as the UFC's President and CEO.

## What is the Octagon?

The Octagon is the eight-sided fighting arena used for every UFC bout. It is
enclosed by a fenced cage rather than ropes, which allows fighters to strike,
clinch, and grapple against the fence itself.

## Where are UFC events held?

The UFC holds events worldwide, with a large concentration in Las Vegas,
Nevada (home to the UFC Apex training/event facility), but also regularly
hosts cards across the United States, Canada, the UK, Europe, the Middle East
(notably Abu Dhabi), Australia, and Asia.

## How can I watch UFC events?

As of 2026, UFC events are broadcast in the United States on Paramount+,
following a major broadcast rights deal. Availability varies by country and
event tier, and this changes periodically as media rights deals are renewed —
check local listings for the current arrangement in your region.
