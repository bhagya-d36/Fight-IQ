# UFC Weight Classes

## What are the men's weight classes?

The UFC's men's divisions, from lightest to heaviest, are: Flyweight (up to
125 lb / 56.7 kg), Bantamweight (up to 135 lb / 61.2 kg), Featherweight (up to
145 lb / 65.8 kg), Lightweight (up to 155 lb / 70.3 kg), Welterweight (up to
170 lb / 77.1 kg), Middleweight (up to 185 lb / 83.9 kg), Light Heavyweight
(up to 205 lb / 93.0 kg), and Heavyweight (up to 265 lb / 120.2 kg).

## What are the women's weight classes?

The UFC's women's divisions are: Strawweight (up to 115 lb / 52.2 kg),
Flyweight (up to 125 lb / 56.7 kg), Bantamweight (up to 135 lb / 61.2 kg), and
Featherweight (up to 145 lb / 65.8 kg). The women's featherweight division has
been largely inactive since former two-division champion Amanda Nunes
stepped away from competition.

## What is a catchweight fight?

A catchweight is a bout contracted at a weight limit different from any
standard division limit, usually because one or both fighters cannot make an
existing weight class, or a short-notice replacement can't cut to the
original number. Catchweight fights do not affect divisional rankings unless
specifically arranged as a title fight.

## How do weigh-ins and weight cutting work?

Fighters must weigh in at or below their division's limit, typically the day
before the event, and are given a rehydration window to recover weight
before fighting. Many fighters intentionally dehydrate ("cut weight") in the
final days before weigh-ins to compete against smaller opponents, then
rehydrate afterward. Missing weight usually results in a financial penalty to
the offending fighter and forfeiting the ability to win a title in that bout.

## What happens if a fighter misses weight?

If a fighter misses weight, the bout can still proceed as a catchweight
non-title fight, with the fighter who missed weight typically forfeiting a
percentage of their purse to their opponent. If it's a title fight, the
title is no longer on the line for the fighter who missed weight, even if
they win.
