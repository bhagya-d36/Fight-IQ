# UFC and MMA FAQ

## What's the difference between UFC and MMA?

MMA (Mixed Martial Arts) is the sport itself. The UFC (Ultimate Fighting
Championship) is a specific company and promotion that organizes and hosts
MMA events — the largest and most well-known one, but not the only one.
Other MMA promotions include Bellator, PFL, ONE Championship, and RIZIN.

## What is the "BMF" title?

The BMF ("Baddest Mother F*****") title is a novelty, non-sanctioning belt
the UFC introduced in 2019 to reward exceptionally exciting, high-stakes
fights between top fighters, independent of the standard divisional rankings
and title picture. It carries prestige but does not affect a fighter's
divisional ranking or path to a real title shot.

## How are title challengers chosen?

Challengers are typically the top-ranked available contender in a division,
based on recent performance, activity, and the UFC's own matchmaking
decisions. Winning a number-one contender fight, a title eliminator bout, or
putting together a significant win streak against ranked opponents are
common ways a fighter earns a title shot.

## What is a takedown?

A takedown is a wrestling or grappling technique used to bring a standing
opponent to the ground, typically to gain a dominant position for follow-up
strikes or a submission attempt.

## What is a guard in MMA?

"Guard" refers to a ground grappling position where one fighter is on their
back with their legs wrapped around or positioned against the opponent, used
both defensively (to prevent strikes and control distance) and offensively
(to attack with submissions or sweeps).

## What is ground-and-pound?

Ground-and-pound is a strategy where a fighter, after gaining a dominant
position on the ground (such as full mount or side control), strikes their
opponent with punches or elbows rather than immediately attempting a
submission.

## What is a submission?

A submission is a fight-ending grappling technique — either a choke that cuts
off blood flow or air, or a joint lock that threatens injury — that forces an
opponent to "tap out" (signal surrender) or verbally submit, ending the fight
immediately.

## What does it mean when a fighter is "on a win streak"?

A win streak refers to the number of consecutive fights a fighter has won
without a loss or no-contest in between. Long win streaks, especially against
ranked opposition, are a common path to a title shot.

## What is "The Ultimate Fighter" (TUF)?

"The Ultimate Fighter" is a long-running reality television competition
series where aspiring or established fighters compete in an elimination
tournament while training and living together, coached by current UFC
stars. Its 2005 debut season is widely credited with a major boost in the
UFC's mainstream popularity in the United States.
