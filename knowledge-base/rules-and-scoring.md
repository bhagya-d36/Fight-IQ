# UFC Rules and Scoring

## How can a fighter win a UFC fight?

A fighter can win by: Knockout (KO), when an opponent is rendered unable to
continue by a strike; Technical Knockout (TKO), when the referee, a doctor,
or a fighter's corner stops the fight because a fighter cannot intelligently
defend themselves; Submission, when a fighter taps out or verbally submits
due to a choke or joint lock, or when they are rendered unconscious by a
choke; or Decision, when the fight goes the scheduled distance and judges
score the result.

## How long does a UFC fight last?

Regular (non-title) fights are scheduled for three rounds of five minutes
each, with a one-minute rest period between rounds. Title fights and main
events are scheduled for five rounds of five minutes each.

## How does UFC judging work?

Judges use the 10-Point Must System. The winner of a round is typically
awarded 10 points, and the loser 9 or fewer, based on effective striking,
grappling, control, and aggression. A very close round can still be scored
10-9. A round with a knockdown or clear domination can be scored 10-8, and
extreme domination can be scored 10-7. After all rounds, the judge with the
higher total for each fighter determines that judge's scorecard, and a
majority of the three judges' scorecards determines the winner.

## What are the different types of decisions?

A Unanimous Decision means all three judges scored the fight for the same
fighter. A Split Decision means two judges scored for one fighter and one
judge scored for the other. A Majority Decision means two judges scored for
the same fighter and the third judge scored the fight a draw.

## What moves are illegal in the UFC?

Illegal fouls include: eye gouging, groin strikes, strikes to the back of the
head or spine, strikes to the throat, small joint manipulation (e.g., bending
individual fingers or toes), biting, hair pulling, fish-hooking, headbutts,
and stomping or kicking the head of a grounded opponent in some
jurisdictions. Fighters can be penalized a point, or disqualified for
repeated or egregious fouls.

## What is a "no contest"?

A "no contest" is a result declared when a fight cannot continue or be fairly
judged due to circumstances outside normal competition, most commonly an
accidental foul (like an eye poke or low blow) that injures a fighter badly
enough that they cannot continue, or when a result is later overturned (e.g.,
due to a failed drug test). A no contest does not count as a win, loss, or
draw for either fighter, and does not change title status.

## What is a "Performance of the Night" or "Fight of the Night" bonus?

These are discretionary bonus payments the UFC awards to fighters after an
event for standout individual performances ("Performance of the Night") or
for the most exciting fight on the card ("Fight of the Night"), in addition
to a fighter's contracted purse.
